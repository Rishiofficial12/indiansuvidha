ASKED QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about Indian Police Clearance Certificates? Check out our FAQs for quick and clear answers to all your queries!

What is an Indian Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) and why do I need it?

An Indian PCC is an official document issued by the Indian government, stating that the applicant has no criminal record within India. It is often required for various purposes, including immigration to foreign countries, obtaining a work visa, pursuing higher education abroad, or adopting a child.

What documents are required to apply for an Indian PCC from Australia?

Typically, you will need your original Indian passport, proof of current address in Australia, a copy of your visa/residency permit, and photographs. The specific requirements can vary based on your visa type and purpose, and our team will provide a precise checklist.

How long does it take to get an Indian PCC?

The processing time for an Indian PCC can vary depending on the Indian High Commission/Consulate's workload and whether police verification is required in India. Generally, it can take several weeks to a couple of months. We will keep you updated on your application status.

Can I apply for an Indian PCC online from overseas?

Yes, Bharat Suvidha specializes in assisting Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and foreign nationals with their Indian PCC applications from overseas. Our service is designed to be fully online, minimizing the need for physical visits to any office or embassy.

Is police verification required for PCC applications from Australia?

For PCC applications from overseas, police verification in India may or may not be required, depending on factors like your last residential address in India and the duration since you left. Our experts will assess your case and inform you if police verification is likely needed and guide you through that process.
