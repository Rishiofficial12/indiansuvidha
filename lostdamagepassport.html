ASKED QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about replacing a lost or damaged Indian passport? Find quick answers here!

What should I do first if my Indian passport is lost in Australia?

The immediate steps are to file a police report regarding the loss. This report is crucial for your application for a new passport. Once the report is filed, contact Bharat Suvidha for online assistance with the replacement process.

What documents are required to apply for a replacement passport?

You will typically need the police report (for lost passports), a copy of your old passport (if available), proof of address in Australia, a visa copy, a copy of your foreign passport (if applicable), and a completed application form. Our team will provide a comprehensive checklist and assist in preparing all necessary documents.

How long does it take to get a new passport after applying for a lost/damaged one?

The processing time can vary based on the Indian High Commission/Consulate's current workload and the specific circumstances of your case (e.g., if police verification is required). Generally, it can take several weeks. We will keep you updated on your application status throughout the process.

Can I travel with a damaged passport?

It is strongly advised not to travel with a damaged passport, as it may be deemed invalid by immigration authorities. You should apply for a replacement passport as soon as possible if your passport is damaged.

Does Bharat Suvidha help with filing the police report?

While we cannot directly file the police report for you (as it requires personal presence for reporting the loss/theft), we provide clear guidance on how to obtain the necessary police report, including what information to include and which authority to contact, ensuring you fulfill this crucial step correctly.
